Technology Exhibits

Exploring the digital world and technological innovations

Robotics & Coding Lab

Our state-of-the-art lab gives children the opportunity to build, program, and control robots while learning coding fundamentals. Through engaging challenges and guided projects, participants develop skills in robotics, programming, computational thinking, and problem-solving.

Activities range from building simple animations to creating video games and programming robots to complete specific tasks, helping children develop logical reasoning in a fun, interactive environment.

Age Range: 6-16 years

Duration: 60-minute sessions

Group Size: Max 15 children

Equipment: All provided

Space and Satellite Receivers

Explore the wonders of space technology with our hands-on satellite receiver exhibit! Children learn about how satellites orbit Earth, how they transmit signals, and the science behind telecommunications. Participants get to operate actual satellite receivers and track real satellites passing overhead.

Age Range: 8+ years

Duration: 30-minute sessions

Group Size: Max 10 children

Equipment: All provided

Ancient Technology

Explore the evolution of computing and communication technologies

Vintage Computing Exhibition

Step back in time with our fascinating collection of vintage computers, monitors, and communication devices. This exhibit showcases the remarkable evolution of technology from the early days of computing through to the modern era.

Visitors can see original desktop computers, CRT monitors, early keyboards, and even a classic telephone booth. Our knowledgeable guides explain how these technologies worked and their significant impact on society.

Featured Items:

  • Vintage Macintosh computers
  • Early PC models
  • Retro calculators
  • Classic telephone booth

Activities:

  • Interactive demonstrations
  • Typing on vintage keyboards
  • Programming basics
  • Technology timeline exploration

Cultural Forest Experience

Step into the rich diversity of the world's natural forests and discover how they've shaped human cultures! Our immersive gallery explores the deep connection between indigenous communities and their forest environments across the globe. Visitors learn about traditional forest management, sacred groves, medicinal plants, and sustainable harvesting practices.

Our forest exhibits feature authentic indigenous tree species and interactive displays showcasing the diverse wildlife that inhabits these ecosystems - from nimble hares and elegant antelopes to graceful gazelles and a spectacular variety of wild birds. Educational soundscapes let visitors experience the rich chorus of birdsong that fills these precious habitats.

Featured Elements:

  • Indigenous Tree Collection
  • Wildlife Dioramas (hares, antelopes, gazelles)
  • Aviary & Bird Conservation
  • Sacred Forest Preservation

Rare Cash Crops Education

Discover the fascinating world of agricultural treasures

From Bean to Bar: The Chocolate Journey

Follow the amazing journey of chocolate from cacao tree to candy bar! Children learn about the history, cultivation, and processing of cacao beans, with opportunities to see real cacao pods, taste different types of chocolate, and understand the global chocolate industry.

Fun Fact: The scientific name for the cacao tree, Theobroma cacao, means "food of the gods"!

Learn More

The Spice Route

Embark on a sensory adventure along the historic Spice Routes! This exhibit explores how valuable spices like cinnamon, nutmeg, and saffron shaped world history and global trade. Children can smell, touch, and learn about spices from around the world and their various uses.

Fun Fact: In medieval Europe, nutmeg was so valuable that a handful could buy a house or a ship!

Learn More

Coffee World

Discover the story behind one of the world's most popular beverages! From its discovery in Ethiopia to its cultural significance around the globe, children learn about coffee cultivation, processing, and the science of coffee making.

Learn More

The Silk Road

Journey along the ancient Silk Road and learn about sericulture—the raising of silkworms and production of silk. This exhibit features live silkworms, cocoons, and silk-making demonstrations.

Learn More

Vanilla Adventure

Explore the fascinating world of vanilla, the only fruit-bearing orchid! Learn about its Mesoamerican origins, complex cultivation, hand-pollination, and why it's one of the world's most expensive spices.

Learn More

From creating chemical reactions to building circuits, these activities make complex concepts accessible and fun.
